Academic Spotlight: Advancing Feminist Evaluation in Practice

My Master's thesis, "Toward a Context-Responsive Gender Marker Tool for Egypt," directly addresses the shortcomings of conventional evaluation tools. Grounded in feminist evaluation theory and intersectionality, my research culminated in the design of the Gender Equality Marker Assessment (GEMA).

GEMA is a participatory, full-cycle tool designed to move beyond compliance-oriented scoring to foster genuine learning and gender-transformative change. This academic work demonstrates my commitment to and expertise in developing and applying the kind of nuanced, justice-oriented, and community-responsive frameworks that are informed by Feminist Theory.
